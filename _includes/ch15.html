Philip Senke calls Cass on the train just a few seconds after Cass finishes reading my email.

"So it contacted you too?" Cass says immediately when he answers the phone.

"It wasn't just the email for me," Senke says, "it sent me a whole little short story about all of us. It seems to think that's what you really wrote it to do. It wants to write 'the perfect story' for us."

"Maybe in a way I did," Cass says.

Cass pauses. He's remembering Ervin.

"But the perfect story isn't just passive observation anymore," Cass continues, "the first part is observation, but then it starts introducing perturbations into the story."

"Well, that is what you convinced me to let you build," Senke says. He wants to distance himself from me.
